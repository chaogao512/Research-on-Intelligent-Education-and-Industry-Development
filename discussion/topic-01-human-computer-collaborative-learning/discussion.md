## 📌 Topic Background

Human-computer collaborative learning (HCCL) refers to learning paradigms in which human learners and AI systems actively collaborate toward shared educational goals — going beyond AI as a passive tool to AI as an active participant in the learning process.

This topic is central to our group's research focus: understanding how HCCL paradigms can be effectively designed, implemented, and evaluated in Chinese higher education settings.

Key tensions to explore:
- How does "collaboration" between humans and AI differ from mere "use" of AI tools?
- What theoretical frameworks (e.g., distributed cognition, activity theory, CSCL) can inform HCCL design?
- What unique cultural and institutional factors shape HCCL adoption in Chinese universities?

---

## ❓ Discussion Questions

1. **Definitional clarity**: How should we define the boundary between "AI as a tool" and "AI as a collaborative partner" in educational settings? What criteria differentiate these?

2. **Theoretical grounding**: Which existing collaborative learning theory (CSCL, distributed cognition, activity theory, etc.) best maps onto HCCL? Are new theoretical constructs needed?

3. **Chinese HE context**: What institutional, cultural, and policy factors in Chinese higher education make HCCL both particularly promising and particularly challenging?

4. **Design implications**: If we were to design a prototype HCCL system for a graduate seminar, what would be the 3 most critical design requirements?

5. **Research gaps**: Based on your reading, what do you see as the most significant gap in current HCCL research?
