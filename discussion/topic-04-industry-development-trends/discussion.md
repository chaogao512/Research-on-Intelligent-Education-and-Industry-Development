## 📌 Topic Background

The intelligent education industry in China has undergone dramatic transformation: the 2021 "Double Reduction" policy (双减) reshaped the K-12 tutoring market, forcing EdTech companies to pivot toward higher education, vocational training, and enterprise learning. Meanwhile, the integration of AI foundation models is creating new product categories and business models.

Understanding these industry dynamics is essential for researchers who want their work to have real-world impact.

---

## ❓ Discussion Questions

1. **Post-双减 landscape**: How has the Chinese EdTech industry adapted to the Double Reduction policy? What new market segments have emerged?

2. **Foundation model integration**: How are Chinese EdTech companies integrating foundation models (e.g., Qwen, ERNIE, Baichuan) into their products? What are the key technical and business challenges?

3. **Research-industry gap**: What types of academic research in intelligent education are most likely to be adopted by industry? What creates the disconnect between research and commercial deployment?

4. **HE market opportunity**: What specific opportunities exist in the Chinese higher education technology market (digital campuses, graduate education, professional development)?

5. **Career pathways**: For researchers specializing in HCCL and intelligent education, what career trajectories are most viable: academia, large EdTech companies, startups, or government/policy roles?
