## 📌 Topic Background

The rapid adoption of generative AI tools — particularly large language model-based tools like ChatGPT, Copilot, Doubao, and Qwen — has fundamentally disrupted traditional higher education practices. Faculty, students, and administrators in Chinese universities are navigating a rapidly evolving landscape with limited institutional guidance.

This discussion explores how AI tools are actually being used (and misused) in Chinese higher education, and what this means for pedagogy, assessment, and research practice.

---

## ❓ Discussion Questions

1. **Student use patterns**: What patterns of LLM tool use are you observing among graduate students in your institution? How does this differ from undergraduate use?

2. **Academic integrity**: How should universities define and enforce academic integrity policies in an era of capable generative AI? Is "AI-generated text" detection technically reliable?

3. **Pedagogical redesign**: Which traditional assessment formats (essays, problem sets, oral exams, etc.) remain meaningful in the presence of capable AI tools? How should pedagogy change?

4. **Faculty attitudes**: What are the dominant faculty attitudes toward student AI tool use in your institutional context? What are the key drivers of acceptance or resistance?

5. **Productive integration**: Can you describe a specific scenario where AI tool use genuinely enhanced a student's learning outcome, rather than substituting for learning effort?
