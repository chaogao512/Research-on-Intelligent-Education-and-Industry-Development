## 📌 Topic Background

As intelligent education systems collect increasingly rich data about learners — behavioral logs, performance records, physiological signals, social interactions — the question of how this data should be governed becomes critical.

China's Personal Information Protection Law (PIPL, 2021) places strict requirements on the collection and use of personal data, with significant implications for educational AI systems. At the same time, rich data is often essential for building effective personalized learning systems.

This discussion explores the tension between data utility and data governance in intelligent education.

---

## ❓ Discussion Questions

1. **Regulatory landscape**: How does China's PIPL framework apply to university learning management systems and intelligent tutoring systems? What are the key compliance requirements?

2. **Consent in education**: Given the power imbalance between students and institutions, is meaningful informed consent for educational data collection truly achievable? How should systems be designed to address this?

3. **Data minimization vs. personalization**: Effective personalization often requires rich data. How can we design systems that balance data minimization principles with the need for personalization?

4. **Algorithmic accountability**: Who is responsible when an AI-driven educational recommendation produces a poor outcome for a student? How should accountability be allocated?

5. **Governance frameworks**: What would an ideal educational data governance framework look like for a Chinese university implementing an HCCL system?
