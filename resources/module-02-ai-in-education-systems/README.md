## 🎯 Learning Objectives

By the end of this module, students should be able to:

1. Explain how large language models (LLMs) are being applied in education settings.
2. Describe the architecture and components of modern intelligent tutoring systems.
3. Evaluate the strengths and limitations of AI-driven adaptive learning platforms.
4. Analyze real-world AI-in-education deployments in Chinese higher education institutions.
5. Identify ethical considerations in deploying AI systems in academic environments.

## 🔑 Key Concepts

- **Large Language Models (LLMs)**: Foundation models (GPT, LLaMA, Qwen, etc.) applied to tutoring, feedback generation, and question answering.
- **Knowledge Tracing**: Modeling and predicting student knowledge states over time.
- **Automated Essay Scoring (AES)**: AI systems that grade free-text student responses.
- **Dialogue-based Tutoring**: Conversational AI used for Socratic-style instructional dialogue.
- **Multimodal Learning Analytics**: Combining text, speech, gesture, and physiological signals.
