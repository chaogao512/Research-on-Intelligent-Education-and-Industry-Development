## 🎯 Learning Objectives

By the end of this module, students should be able to:

1. Define human-computer collaborative learning (HCCL) and situate it within broader CSCL (Computer-Supported Collaborative Learning) theory.
2. Distinguish HCCL paradigms: AI-as-peer, AI-as-tutor, AI-as-tool, and AI-as-co-creator.
3. Analyze the unique challenges and opportunities of HCCL in Chinese higher education contexts.
4. Design a conceptual HCCL system for a specific learning scenario.
5. Critically evaluate empirical studies of HCCL effectiveness.

## 🔑 Key Concepts

- **HCCL (Human-Computer Collaborative Learning)**: Learning paradigms where human learners and AI systems actively collaborate toward shared learning goals.
- **AI-as-Peer**: AI agents that work alongside students as equal partners in problem-solving.
- **AI-as-Tutor**: AI systems that guide students through learning tasks with feedback and scaffolding.
- **Cognitive Scaffolding**: Temporary support structures provided by AI to help learners develop higher-order thinking.
- **Shared Mental Models**: The degree to which human and AI collaborators have aligned representations of a task.
- **Chinese HE Context**: Unique factors including large class sizes, Confucian learning culture, policy environment, and digital infrastructure.
